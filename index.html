Managing Director's Message Forward

It is my privilege and pleasure to express my view of concern for the unique Quick Engineering & Construction which is established for the year 2000 and beyond. Centuries have passed without any clue of better living in terms of Adequate, Accessible and Quality Water Supply and Sanitation in rural Villages throughout PNG.

The consequences of low access to water and sanitation and poor hygiene practices are well documented and evident in terms of increased incidences of water borne diseases, most notably diarrhoea, typhoid etc. cholera leading to higher morbidity and death, poorer educational attainment and economic impacts at both the household and national level.

Access to clean, safe and adequate water supply is basic human needs and rights. The benefits of improved water supply in rural areas are many, including prevention of disease, improved basic health care, better nutrition and etc.

The objective of the QEC is to provide equitable access to safe, convenient and sustainable water supply and sanitation and to promote improved hygiene practices and long term hygiene behavior change at the personal, household, community and institutional level particular to rural and urban settlement areas that are currently under-served.

QEC sees that water PNG cannot provide clean and adequate water supply and sanitation in all rural areas in PNG so we take an initiative to conduct feasibility studies, site investigations, design, costings and installation of different types of water supply systems in collaboration with:

  • Providing assistance to rural WaSH facilitation and implementation organisations, including provincial, district and local level administrations, provincial health offices, NGOs and the private sector to support the planning and implementation of water, sanitation and health infrastructure at rural community level.
  • Assisting in the development of water and sanitation investment and service support plans to be submitted to local and district level government authorities.
  • Ensuring Service Improvement Program (SIP) support includes sustainable WaSH activities.
  • Working with development partners to increase finance and support rural WaSH development initiatives.
  • Working with the SOEs and other operators to identify opportunities to extend existing urban service areas to surrounding communities and identify other support that utilities can provide to rural communities.
